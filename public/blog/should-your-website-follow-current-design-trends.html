Should your website follow current design trends?

Certain trends in web-design have become so strong that many websites have started to look the same. This is in part fuelled by the use of styling frameworks such as Bootstrap, TailwindCSS, etc. Some people may find this dull, but it is also a great opportunity to get a website with a proven structure and design.

So, should your website follow the current design trends, or should you aim for an original website design?

How Strong is Your Brand?

Do you produce a unique product or do your customers Google your name and know your product beforehand? Then you should definitely try to "wow" them with an original design. This will make their user experience memorable and increase their perception of your product and your brand.

Most companies do not fit in this category and should therefore focus on:

  1. Website performance (both loading time and SEO)
  2. Ease of use
  3. Short and precise texts
  4. Awesome images and video

The strong design trends we see all emphasize those four elements, such as the use of vector images and animations that provides your website with a clean user-interface and a site that loads fast. We also see strong trends in the way a website is structured, i.e. in the hero section, the feature section, and contact.

While some designers may find this dull, these trends provide the end-users with familiar fonts, colours, and site structure that allows them to navigate with ease and thereby improving their user-experience. In the end, it is user-experience that should guide design-decisions when building a website for a company.

More Advantages of Following Design Trends

Besides high-performance, SEO and user-familiarity you can often get more for less. If you opt for using a design framework, the development of your website can be faster or cheaper because. This is because many of the design elements have already been decided by following the trend.

Fonts, colours, sizing, and text is what can make your website personal. It might still have a tendency to "feel" like other websites, which might not be a bad thing for your business.

In Conclusion

In most cases, I believe that companies should opt for following the design trends when deciding on a new website. With so many different design frameworks available today you can still get a website that feels uniquely yours while looking familiar to the visitors.
